Welcome to Day 8!!

Today we will learn about messages and links from unknown senders, and how to determine if it is legitimate or not.

When these links are clicked, it could immediately start installing a virus or steal your data.

Here we can see a text messsage sent supposedly from USPS, asking us to verify our address by clicking on an unknown link.

Immediately, we can see that the email from this sender is suspicious and has nothing to do with USPS

Next, the link given to us is unordinary, using the ".top" domain address.

These little things are clear indicators that the message/email/call is harmful and should immediately be blocked.

Here are some common tactics that scammers will use to trick people:

FEAR: By scaring the receiver, they will pressure the victim to give out their personal information.

URGENCY: They will tell the receiver that they need to take a certain action or else something bad will happen.

GREED: They will say that the receiver won an award or prize and only needs to take one more step in order to win.

REMINDER: Always be cautious when sent an unknown message or email, and always take any means necessary to ensure that it is safe.
